# Target Users

## Primary Audiences

- Students learning to manage limited budgets
- Working professionals managing salary and household spending
- Freelancers, consultants, and gig workers with irregular income
- Self-employed users tracking simple personal and work-related cash flow
- Families and couples managing shared household finances
- Homemakers and household budget managers
- People with multiple income sources
- First-time budgeters improving financial literacy

## Secondary Audiences

- Retirees and senior citizens who need clear, accessible finance tracking
- Small business owners who need simple income, expense, and category tracking

NidhiFlow is not a replacement for business accounting, payroll, invoicing,
tax, lending, brokerage, or investment-management software.

## User Needs

### New or Guest User

- Start immediately without registration
- Understand what data is local and what requires an account
- Record transactions, budgets, bills, and goals
- See useful summaries and reports
- Export local data

### Individual Account User

- Back up and restore history
- Synchronize across devices
- Save preferences and receive notifications
- Upload attachments and use server-generated reports
- Access persistent personalized AI in later phases

### Family Member

- Join one shared family workspace
- View all shared workspace data
- Add and edit shared transactions
- Collaborate on budgets and goals
- Understand who changed shared data and when

### Younger User

- Use an attractive, non-boring interface
- Learn through short lessons, quizzes, and practical tips
- Personalize themes, avatars, dashboard layouts, and goal visuals
- Opt into healthy challenges, badges, streaks, and milestones

### Accessibility-Sensitive User

- Navigate by keyboard and assistive technology
- Understand charts without relying on color
- Use readable contrast, labels, touch targets, and reduced motion
- Choose language, currency, theme, and notification preferences

## Inclusive Product Rules

- Do not assume financial expertise.
- Do not require an account for locally feasible features.
- Do not assume INR despite INR examples in mockups.
- Do not shame users for spending, debt, missed streaks, or incomplete goals.
- Do not make serious financial content childish.
- Do not use age, income, family status, or occupation to provide discriminatory
  treatment.
